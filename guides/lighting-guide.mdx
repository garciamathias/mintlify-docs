## The Importance of Lighting

Lighting is crucial for highlighting texture, shape, and finish. Master these techniques for both capturing products and generating scenes.

<Info>
  **Why it matters**: Proper lighting boosts both realism and appeal, making products irresistible to customers.
</Info>

## Creating Product Shots

### Natural Light Photography

The gold standard for product photography:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Setup" icon="sun">
    - Position near large windows
    - Use north-facing for consistency
    - Avoid direct sunlight
    - Best times: morning/late afternoon
  </Card>
  <Card title="Benefits" icon="sparkles">
    - Soft, diffused illumination
    - Authentic textures revealed
    - Minimal harsh shadows
    - True color representation
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Artificial Lighting Setup

When natural light isn't available:

| Tool | Purpose | Setup Tips |
|------|---------|------------|
| **Softboxes** | Even, shadow-free light | Place at 45° angles |
| **Diffusers** | Soften harsh light | Between light and product |
| **Reflectors** | Fill shadow areas | Opposite main light |
| **LED Panels** | Consistent color | Match daylight temp |

### Directional Control

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Side Lighting">
    **Purpose**: Emphasize texture & depth
    - Position light at 45-90° angle
    - Creates dramatic shadows
    - Highlights surface details
    - Best for textured materials
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Front Lighting">
    **Purpose**: Even illumination
    - Minimizes shadows
    - Shows true colors
    - Good for flat lays
    - Safe, versatile option
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Backlighting">
    **Purpose**: Create separation
    - Adds halo effect
    - Separates from background
    - Creates depth
    - Dramatic for translucent materials
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Fill Lighting">
    **Purpose**: Balance shadows
    - Softens harsh contrasts
    - Reveals shadow details
    - Creates natural look
    - Essential for realism
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Color Temperature Mastery

Match your lighting to your brand aesthetic:

| Temperature | Kelvin | Atmosphere | Best For |
|-------------|--------|------------|----------|
| **Warm White** | 2700-3000K | Cozy, inviting | Traditional, rustic |
| **Neutral White** | 3500-4000K | Balanced, natural | Versatile option |
| **Cool White** | 5000-5500K | Clean, modern | Contemporary designs |
| **Daylight** | 6000-6500K | Crisp, energetic | True color matching |

<Warning>
  Always maintain consistent color temperature across all lights to avoid mixed lighting issues.
</Warning>

## Generating Lifestyle Images

### Communicating Lighting in Prompts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Describe the Mood">
    Use atmospheric terms: "soft morning light", "dramatic sunset", "bright and airy"
  </Step>
  <Step title="Specify Sources">
    Mention windows, lamps, or natural light directions
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload Inspiration">
    Use reference images to convey exact lighting style
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refine Results">
    Use Relight tool for post-generation adjustments
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Effective Lighting Prompts

**Morning Light**
```
"Soft morning sunlight streaming through sheer curtains, 
warm golden hour glow, gentle shadows"
```

**Afternoon Brightness**
```
"Bright natural daylight from large windows, 
even illumination, minimal shadows, airy feeling"
```

**Evening Ambiance**
```
"Warm evening lighting with table lamps, 
cozy atmosphere, soft pools of light, intimate mood"
```

**Studio Lighting**
```
"Professional studio lighting, bright and even, 
no harsh shadows, product showcase quality"
```

### Managing Light Intensity

If generated lighting is too intense:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Reduce in Prompt">
    - Change "floor-to-ceiling windows" to "small window"
    - Remove multiple light sources
    - Add "soft", "diffused", "subtle"
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Post-Generation Fix">
    - Use Relight tool
    - Try Low setting for subtle light
    - Generate variations
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting Lighting Issues

### Common Problems & Solutions

| Issue | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Too Dark** | Insufficient light sources | Add windows or lamps to prompt |
| **Overexposed** | Too many/bright sources | Reduce light sources, use "soft" |
| **Mixed Colors** | Multiple temperatures | Specify single color temp |
| **Harsh Shadows** | Direct lighting | Add "diffused" or "soft" |
| **Flat Lighting** | Too even | Add directional elements |
