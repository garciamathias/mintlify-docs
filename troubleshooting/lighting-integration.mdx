## Lighting Harmony

Proper lighting integration ensures your products look naturally placed within generated scenes, not pasted on top.

<Info>
  **Key Principle**: Match the intensity and direction of light between your product and the generated environment.
</Info>

## Understanding Light Matching

### Why Lighting Consistency Matters

Mismatched lighting immediately signals "fake" to viewers:
- Conflicting shadows reveal compositing
- Different color temperatures clash
- Intensity variations look unnatural
- Trust and quality perception suffer

### The Integration Challenge

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Common Issues">
    - Product too bright for dim room
    - Shadow directions don't match
    - Color temperature conflicts
    - Harsh product lighting in soft scene
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Visual Impact">
    - Product appears "pasted on"
    - Scene loses cohesion
    - Unprofessional result
    - Reduced conversion rates
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Matching Light Direction

### Reading Shadow Cues

<Warning>
  Shadow direction is the most critical factor for believable integration.
</Warning>

**How to analyze:**
1. Identify main light source in scene
2. Note shadow direction from objects
3. Check your product's shadows
4. Ensure alignment

### Common Scenarios

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Window Light">
    **Scene setup:**
    - Light from left/right
    - Long shadows
    - Gradient falloff
    
    **Product needs:**
    - Matching side lighting
    - Similar shadow length
    - Gradual light falloff
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Overhead Lighting">
    **Scene setup:**
    - Ceiling fixtures
    - Short shadows
    - Even distribution
    
    **Product needs:**
    - Top-down lighting
    - Minimal shadows
    - Uniform brightness
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Multiple Sources">
    **Scene setup:**
    - Windows + lamps
    - Complex shadows
    - Mixed temperatures
    
    **Product needs:**
    - Balanced lighting
    - Soft shadows
    - Neutral temperature
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Color Temperature Harmony

### Understanding Color Temperature

| Temperature | Kelvin | Visual | Scene Type |
|-------------|--------|--------|------------|
| **Warm** | 2700-3000K | Orange/yellow | Cozy, evening |
| **Neutral** | 3500-4500K | White | Balanced, natural |
| **Cool** | 5000-6500K | Blue/white | Modern, daylight |

### Fixing Temperature Mismatches

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Product too warm">
    **Symptoms**: Yellow/orange tint vs cool scene
    
    **Solutions**:
    - Adjust original photo white balance
    - Use cooler scene descriptions
    - Add "cool daylight" to prompt
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Product too cool">
    **Symptoms**: Blue tint vs warm scene
    
    **Solutions**:
    - Warm up source image
    - Generate warmer scenes
    - Use "warm ambient light" in prompt
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Advanced Integration Techniques

### Multi-Product Lighting

When staging multiple products:

1. **Standardize source lighting**: Shoot all products under same conditions
2. **Apply uniform Relight**: Use same setting for all items
3. **Check shadow consistency**: All shadows should point same direction
4. **Verify color match**: No item should stand out

### Scene-Specific Adjustments

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Bright Scenes" icon="sun">
    - Start with well-lit products
    - Use High relight sparingly
    - Add "bright, airy" to prompt
    - Minimize shadows
  </Card>
  <Card title="Moody Scenes" icon="moon">
    - Begin with softer product light
    - Apply Low relight
    - Include "soft ambient" in prompt
    - Embrace shadows
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Troubleshooting Guide

### Common Lighting Issues

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Floating appearance** | No shadow connection | Lower product, add contact shadow |
| **Glowing product** | Too bright vs scene | Use lower Relight setting |
| **Muddy colors** | Wrong color temp | Match scene temperature |
| **Harsh contrast** | Direct vs diffused light | Soften with Relight tool |

### Quick Fixes

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Shadow Issues">
    1. Check product placement
    2. Ensure floor contact
    3. Match shadow direction
    4. Use appropriate Relight
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Brightness Problems">
    1. Compare to scene objects
    2. Adjust Relight intensity
    3. Regenerate if needed
    4. Try different times of day
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Color Mismatches">
    1. Identify temperature conflict
    2. Adjust source or scene
    3. Use neutral lighting
    4. Keep consistent
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Best Practices

### Pre-Production

<Info>
  Good lighting integration starts with proper product photography.
</Info>

**Product photography tips:**
- Use diffused, even lighting
- Avoid harsh directional light
- Maintain neutral color temperature
- Create soft shadows

### During Generation

**Prompt strategies:**
- Describe lighting specifically
- Match product photo conditions
- Use consistent terminology
- Reference real scenarios

### Post-Generation

**Quality checks:**
- [ ] Shadow directions align
- [ ] Brightness levels match
- [ ] Colors harmonize
- [ ] No floating effect
- [ ] Natural integration

## Lighting Consistency Checklist

Before finalizing any image:

1. **Shadow check**: All pointing same way?
2. **Brightness test**: Product blend naturally?
3. **Color harmony**: Temperatures match?
4. **Edge quality**: No harsh outlines?
5. **Overall feel**: Looks photographed together?
